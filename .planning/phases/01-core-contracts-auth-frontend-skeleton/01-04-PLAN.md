<objective>
Populate the `@call-it/ui` workspace shell from Plan 01 with the full design-system surface Phase 1 pages consume: primitives (Button, Card, Tag, CornerBrackets, Skeleton with 6 variants, Toast with 3 statuses + countdown drain, Stamp), compound components (Receipt — the multi-mode design lever for the entire receipt critical path, ConvictionBar, CallCard, ProfileHeader), and the `useToast` hook. Every component honors the neobrutalist treatment (color tokens, typography, 2-3px borders, hard offset shadows, 4px corner brackets), uses CVA for variant mapping, leans on Radix where it earns its place (Slider, Toast root, Dialog primitives), and — critically — `<Receipt>` is built flexbox-only with zero wallet-address rendering so Phase 7 OG variants can reuse it via Satori without refactor (Pitfall 15) and AUTH-44 holds.

Purpose: Plans 05/06/08/09 cannot render their pages without these primitives. The decision to spend Phase 1 budget on getting `<Receipt>` right pays back in Phase 2 (Live mode), Phase 4 (Settled mode), Phase 7 (OG card via Satori) — the spec calls this out as the design lever underwriting the receipt-loop story. The ESLint no-display-grid rule (Plan 01) and the receipt-no-address Vitest test in this plan are the anti-drift defenses for that decision.

Output: `@call-it/ui` builds, lints, and tests green; every primitive is exported from the barrel; the `<Receipt>` flexbox tree renders preview/live/settled identically; the 6-variant skeleton, 3-status toast, and conviction slider are ready to drop into Plans 05/06/08/09 with zero additional wiring.
