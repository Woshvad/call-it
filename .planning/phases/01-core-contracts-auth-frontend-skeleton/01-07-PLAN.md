<objective>
Vertical slice E+F: the per-user 5-tx server-side paymaster cap (D-02, Pitfall 14), the Circle USDC Paymaster handoff for tx 6+ (D-04/05, replacing the spec's "user provides ETH" with permit-based USDC gas), the address book + 24h cooldown enforced server-side (D-07/08/09/10, Pitfall 20), and the Privy `auth.linked` webhook with belt-and-suspenders cross-check (Pitfall D). Three relayer routes, one background worker, two frontend hooks, two e2e Playwright tests. After this slice, the paymaster economics + the 24h security gate are operational end-to-end and observable from the operator dashboards.

Purpose: Critical-path step 3 (Sponsored tx) lands here. Without this slice, every userOp falls back to Alchemy's vendor-side enforcement (which we cannot trust per Pitfall 14) and no withdrawal cooldown exists (security regression). The webhook + cross-check pattern (Pitfall D) is the most subtle defense in this slice — without it, a Privy webhook delay creates a 30s window where a freshly-linked auth method can authorize a withdrawal it shouldn't.

Output: A relayer that implements the ERC-7677 (or Alchemy custom) paymaster policy endpoint with confirmed-inclusion-only counter increment; an address book + cooldown system at the relayer pre-tx hook; a Circle USDC Paymaster handoff path in the frontend that activates at tx 6; and Playwright coverage of both the cap handoff and the cooldown bypass attempt.
</objective>
